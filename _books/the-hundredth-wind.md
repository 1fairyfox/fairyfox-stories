A fable of the Salt Road. Basri Yel has caught ninety-nine winds and polished the legend of every one
— which is precisely why the Emir of the windless white house offers him the hundredth: the Unbottled,
the Liar's Wind, the wind no catcher has ever taken. To bottle a wind you must speak its true name into
the glass, and the Unbottled answers only to the thing you will not say. Basri's name for it is forty
years old, and it is not a wind's name at all.

_First person, past tense; cadenced and told-aloud, an old showman working a crowd. Seven chapters.
The full tale — lie, catch, and hook — is in the [blueprint](blueprint/)._
