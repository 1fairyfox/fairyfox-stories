A fable of the Salt Road, told after Okri's — a later tale of the girl who sold the wind, now grown and
keeping the Ninth Waystation the old master left her. Zari deals honestly these days; everyone says so,
and she has taught herself to believe them. So when Hadi Sarraf, the wind-broker of the still city,
offers a standing order — every wind she stocks, at triple price, forever, sold to no one else — she
reads the contract twice, finds no cheating in it anywhere, and signs. Then a girl walks a hundred days
across the waste for the one bottle Zari is no longer allowed to sell.

_First person, past tense; spare, wry, and exact — a fable told like a settled account. Three chapters.
