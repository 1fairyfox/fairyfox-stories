A novelette of the Tidewrack Coast. Maren Voss keeps the returns ledger above the chandlery in
Cape Herring — the record of everything the sea gives back. For nineteen years it has given back
nothing of her drowned brother. Then, one grey morning, it does.

_First person, past tense. Planned at seven chapters; grown a chapter at a time. The complete
plan — including how it ends — is in the [blueprint](blueprint/), openly, for anyone who wants to
know what they're getting into._
