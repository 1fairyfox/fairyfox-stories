Hadi Sarraf is the richest man on the Salt Road and the calmest, and the two facts are the same fact.
He is the broker of the still city of Sabr, where the winds are bought but never sold on — bottled,
shelved, and kept, until the sky over Sabr hangs as flat and windless as a held breath. He came to it
honestly, by his own accounting, and he is not wrong: a storm-wind took his caravan and his people from
him in a single afternoon when he was young, and he has spent every coin since buying the weather up
where it can never take anyone again.

## What he wants (and believes)

- **Wants:** every wind, corked and kept, and a sky that cannot hurt anyone. Safety, total and
  permanent — for himself first, and (he would tell you, and mean it) for the whole Road after.
- **Believes:** that a wind kept is a wind rendered harmless, and that mercy is stillness. He offers
  people not a trick but a genuine kindness as he understands it — the end of risk.

## Why he is not a villain (quite)

Hadi never lies and never cheats. His contracts are square to the last coin; his forfeits are written
plainly and read aloud. He is the protagonist's own hard-won lesson — *a wind in the wrong hands is a
catastrophe* — followed one step too far, into *so let me take them all, where no hand can misuse them.*
He is what caution becomes when it stops being brave.

## Voice

Quiet, courteous, unhurried. Speaks in fair terms and reasonable numbers. Never raises his voice,
because he has never needed to.
