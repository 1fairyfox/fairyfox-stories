Noor is eleven, and she has never once stood in weather. A foundling raised inside the Emir's windless
white house, she grew up in the vault among the bottled winds, and her work is her gift: she knows
every captive wind by the sound it makes against glass at night, and can tell a live bottle from a
dead one with her eyes shut. The Emir sends her along to verify the catch. The winds, for their part,
seem to like her.

## What she wants

- **Wants:** to stand in wind. Once. Real weather, on her face — the thing every person on the Road
  has had for free and she has only ever heard through glass.
- **The trouble:** the man she's been sent to verify is the finest catcher alive and a liar in one
  specific, load-bearing place — and Noor has been listening for false notes her whole life.

## Her place in the story

The foil. She has nothing to protect, so she can say true things at full price — and the moment Basri
lies to her, she hears it, and the trust breaks exactly when he'll need it. She is not a wise child or
a lucky charm; her ear is trained, her honesty is a habit of survival, and what she's owed by the end
of the tale is weather.

## Voice

Direct, unadorned, economical — a child who grew up around one adult and a thousand bottles, asking
the plain question everyone else has agreed not to. (Seen through Basri's narration.)
