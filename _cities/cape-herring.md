Cape Herring is the last real town before the coast gives up and becomes rock. A harbour, a
breakwater the winter keeps rearranging, two churches (one shut), a chandlery with a room above
it where the returns ledger has been kept for longer than anyone living remembers. The fleet is
half what it was. The young leave. The fog does not.

## Places within

- **The chandlery ledger-room** — where the town brings what the sea gives back, to be recorded,
  claimed, or held. The current keeper works here.
- **The breakwater** — the long stone arm of the harbour; the sea returns things to its landward
  side more often than chance should allow.
- **The Ropewalk** — the old net-drying sheds, mostly empty now, where children dare each other
  in the dark.

## Feel

Cold iron and creosote. A town that counts things because it has learned not to trust what stays.
People here are courteous, close-mouthed, and slow to forgive — the coast in miniature.
