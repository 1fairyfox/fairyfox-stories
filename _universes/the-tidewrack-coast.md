A long, grey stretch of northern coast — herring towns, salt-rotted piers, fog that comes in
like a decision. It is our world, bent by one quiet rule: **the sea returns what it takes.**
Not always, not soon, and never quite the same. A lost oar comes back the next morning; a wedding
ring, a year on; sometimes a thing waits a lifetime and arrives unrusted, as though the water kept
it out of spite or mercy — no one can say which.

The people of the coast have made their peace with this the way people make peace with weather:
by keeping records, keeping rituals, keeping their expectations low. Nobody calls it a miracle.
They call it *the return*, and they mean it the way you mean a debt.

## Ground rules

- **The return is real, rare, and unruly.** Objects come back most often; the larger and more
  loved a thing, the longer and stranger its return. It cannot be summoned, only waited for.
- **It is our world otherwise** — no magic systems, no monsters. The uncanny is a single thread,
  used sparingly. Restraint is the rule; the coast is grounded and lived-in.
- **Time is open.** The coast has held fishing families for centuries; books here may sit in any
  era, from oil-lamp winters to the present.

## Texture

Salt, tar, and cold iron. Fog horns. Ledgers and tide tables. Boats named after the drowned. The
sound the sea makes taking the shingle back down the beach — a long, patient hiss.

## Recurring forces

- **The tide itself**, treated less as weather than as a creditor with a long memory.
- **The keepers** — in each town, someone unofficial who records the returns. The job is half
  clerk, half priest, and it tends to fall to the people least able to let go.

## Canon & latitude

Fixed: the return exists and is capricious; the coast is grounded and un-magical otherwise.
Free: towns, eras, families, and exactly what the sea chooses to give back and when.

## Seeds

- A keeper who has waited decades for the sea to return a person, not a thing.
- A child who finds something returned before the person who lost it has died.
- Two towns that keep rival ledgers, and the year their records disagree.
