# Book-notes — The Cinderwick Job (internal)

Private continuity + voice bible. Not published (under `notes/`, excluded from the build).

## Author-persona & style

- **Author-persona:** *the wry street-poet* — noir/hard-boiled sensibility, obsessed with cities,
  class, and nerve; deadpan wit aimed a little at himself (`craft/author-personas.md`).
- **Prose style:** hard-boiled / propulsive on the `style-spectrum` — terse, sensory, wisecracking,
  street-level; momentum over ornament (`craft/style-spectrum.md`). Hold it chapter to chapter.

## Voice (hold across all 7 chapters)

- First person, **past tense**, Juno Vale. Wry, fast, confident; notices exits, tells, and the
  price of everything; deflects feeling with a joke aimed a little at herself. Momentum over
  melancholy — **gripping before sad** (see `craft/genre-range.md`).
- Tone target: *Carmen Sandiego* — adventurous, stylish, dark-as-a-heist (danger, cons, a polite
  villain), **never grim, never gory, never a downer.** Fun with real stakes.

## Continuity facts (locked)

- Juno's buried real name is **Junia Wren** — known to no one; she became Juno Vale 11 years ago
  after a trusted person sold her to the Ordinal. This is her deepest secret and the lever of the plot.
- Setting: **Cinderwick** (Gaslight Reach). The **Understair** = the under-city of tunnels she knows
  by feel; **the Cage** = the Ordinal's auction house; the **Saltmarket** = finders' exchange.
- **Curios** = objects that do one small, rule-bound impossible thing. Ch.1 curio: a brass snuffbox
  that keeps a whispered secret fresh for a year (minor). The MacGuffin: the **Cinderwick
  Nightingale**, a mechanical bird that sings back any sound it has ever heard (introduced Ch.2–3).
- **Magister Crane** — Ordinal fixer, genteel/never raises his voice; wants Juno *useful*, not caught.
  He (via a go-between "client") engineers the whole job to put her in his debt.
- Juno's flaw: **can't refuse a door she's told she can't open.** The card in Ch.1 ("A door only you
  can open. Ask at the Cage.") weaponizes it. She knows it's a trap and goes anyway.

## Arc reminders

- Want: the score that frees her from the Ordinal. Need: to trust one ally out loud instead of
  going solo. Ch.7 resolves by the need (she trusts the rival finder), not by out-thieving Crane.
- The rival finder (Ch.4) — reckless, charming, "better than he should be"; his true loyalty is the
  Ch.6 reveal. Name/design TBD when writing Ch.4 (give him his own want).
