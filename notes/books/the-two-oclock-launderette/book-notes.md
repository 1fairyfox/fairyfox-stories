# Book-notes — The Two-O'Clock Launderette (internal)

- **Author-persona:** Posy Fell — tender miniaturist / comic humanist. **Style:** whimsical,
  lyrical-light; warm, wry, laugh-then-ache. Hold it: gripping-warm, never saccharine.
- **POV:** first person past, Nadia Okafor; notices everyone's small human details but her own.
- **Continuity:** Wishy-Washy launderette, Fenn Street. Hollow hour = 1–2 a.m., small kind magic.
  Ch.1 magic: the launderette rinses worry/fright out (the boy, the colicky baby's blanket).
  Nadia's coat holds her mother's funeral order-of-service in the inside pocket (never removed) —
  the emotional spine. Regulars: one-shirt Thursday man, the Portuguese twins, Bernadette (comes
  for warmth + a Rich Tea biscuit).
- **Arc:** wants to keep others warm; needs to let herself be helped. Ch.2 she lets the wash run;
  Ch.3 she makes the un-made call to her father. Earned, small, warm.
- **Ch.2 (2026-07-11) — locked:** machine 7 can't be stopped (it locks on the spin); the coat comes
  out **warm, clean, and dry**. Pockets, laid out on the folding table, are **entirely other
  people's**: Bernadette's biscuit money + machine change (£3.50), safety pins, a stranger's hair
  elastic, a Rich Tea wrapper, a takeaway flyer kept for its blank back, a biro that doesn't work.
  The order of service (grey card; her mum's name; the crematorium number in blue biro on the back;
  in the pocket **one year and eight months**) survives the wash **perfect and weightless** — the
  hour rinses out the *not-looking*, not the grief. Also in the pocket since **March**: her dad's new
  number, written by her aunt on a receipt at the wake (*"Dad — new no."*). She puts everything back
  out of habit — and is still holding the number at 2 a.m. when the hour ends. (Ch.3 is the call.)
- **Status:** 2 of 3 chapters written (hand-drafted). Next: **ch.3 "Fenn Street, 2 a.m."** — the
  call, then the mending left to daylight. Keep it small; no magical fix.
