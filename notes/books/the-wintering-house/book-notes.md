# Book-notes — The Wintering House (internal)

- **Author-persona:** Cordelia Fenn — gothic dreamer. **Style:** ornate, hushed, long-breathed;
  dread by suggestion, never spectacle/gore (content bar). Hold it: atmospheric, patient, sad.
- **POV:** first person past, Agnes Thorne; formal manners over a proud, lonely, watchful heart.
  She narrates *from afterwards*, to a record, refusing to spare herself ("let it be written
  honestly").
- **Continuity (chapters are canon; this note was corrected 2026-07-13):**
  - Wintering House, on the Blackmoor moor. Every hearth dead but one.
  - **Mrs Hale** — the housekeeper (kind hands, cold face, the sorry-in-advance look). *(The
    original note called her "Mrs Aldous"; the written chapters do not. Aldous is the master's
    Christian name — the note was wrong, the prose is right.)*
  - **Mr Aldous Ravenscar**, ~45, grave, courteous, tired. **Miss Charlotte Ravenscar**, ~40, bright
    and brittle; talks the way other people bar a door.
  - The one warm room on the first landing (the mistress's). Fire never out; ewer changed daily;
    curtain to the second fold only; walnut table never moved; **the book never closed** — open at
    the eleventh sermon, the page browned by years of the same light. Spectacles folded on it.
    Hairbrush hair gone to thread. Bed turned down one side, pillow with a hollow.
  - Supper 8pm, small parlour, **table laid for four** — the fourth place served and cleared
    untouched. Agnes sits at Mr Ravenscar's left.
  - Agnes's own room is cold and always will be. She is "given the keeping" in ch.3 and greets the
    bed each morning.
  - **Ch.4 (new canon):** Charlotte gives Agnes the dead mistress's **grey shawl** — "it's nobody's
    … she'd want it used." The **family piece** hangs at the turn of the stair: old Mr Ravenscar,
    Aldous at 18, Charlotte at 12, and Mrs Ravenscar seated (the only likeness of her face anywhere
    in the house). Behind her chair, painted in later, in three different hands: **Miss Pargeter,
    Miss Vane, Miss Loder** — three companions, hands folded at the waist. Beside Miss Loder, a
    patch of canvas **sized, primed and waiting**. Mrs Hale: *"It don't get bigger. It's always been
    one."* The painter **Sowerby** comes up from Lanthwaite in the spring; the family ask Agnes to
    sit, and Aldous uses her Christian name for the first time. She says yes, knowing exactly what
    she is saying yes to, and cries with happiness.
- **Secret (Ch.8–9):** Mrs Ravenscar died years ago; the family keeps her room/place/fiction alive
  — a shared refusal to grieve — and means Agnes to become the next who tends the warmth and never
  asks. Ending: Agnes grieves honestly, opens the window to the wind, leaves the belonging, keeps herself.
- **Arc:** wants a place/to be kept; needs to refuse the comfortable lie. The haunting = denied grief.
  Her flaw is the fold: she *folds the cold clear part of herself away* rather than ask. It costs her
  nothing to do, and that is the horror.
- **Open thread:** the three earlier companions "went." Where they went is deliberately never
  answered in prose (the book is not a murder story) — the dread is that Agnes never presses.
- **Status:** ch.4 of 11 written (ch.2–4 = "The Comfortable Lie"; ch.5 begins "The Cracks").
