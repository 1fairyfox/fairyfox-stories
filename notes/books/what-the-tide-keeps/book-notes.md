# Book-notes — What the Tide Keeps (internal)

Private continuity + voice bible for writing later chapters. Not published (under `notes/`,
excluded from the Jekyll build). Public-facing world lives in the `_characters` / `_cities` /
`_universes` pages and the `blueprint`.

## Voice (hold this exactly across all 7 chapters)

- First person, **past tense**, Maren Voss. Dry, precise, controlled; counts and weighs things;
  undercounts her own feelings. Long, careful sentences that occasionally break short when the
  feeling gets through. She states facts about the world and lets the emotion sit in the subtext.
- **No sentimentality on the page.** The grief is shown through control, ritual, and what she
  refuses to say — never announced. Restraint is the style (see `craft/voice-and-pov.md`).
- The coast's rule (the return) is treated as mundane fact, never marvelled at.

## Continuity facts (locked)

- Keeper of the Cape Herring returns ledger, 19 years. Predecessors: her father (briefly), and
  before him **Sorrel Aim** (first ~200 pages of the oldest ledger, her hand).
- **Emil** drowned 20 years ago off the **landward side of the breakwater**; the argument was that
  afternoon, Maren started it. Sea returned nothing of his for 19 years — until now.
- Emil tied fisherman's knots **left-handed** ("thought it looked cleverer"); Maren taught him the
  ordinary way. This knot on the oilskin bundle is the first quiet identifier. (Ch.1)
- Ritual: Maren **closes the ledger at noon**; never leaves an unfinished line — until Ch.1, where
  she leaves the 4th line **blank**. That blank line is the book's spine; pay it off in Ch.7.
- The returned object is **Emil's clasp-knife**, initials she scratched into the handle at nine,
  **no rust** (revealed Ch.2; Osk foreshadows "no rust" in Ch.1).
- **Osk Berentsen** — careful far-side fisherman, never brought a return before; found the bundle
  above the tide line, won't say whose it is. Reliable about the sea.
- **Halvard Munn** — lost his wife to the sea in the spring, nothing returned; visits, doesn't say
  her name. A mirror/foil for Maren's waiting.
