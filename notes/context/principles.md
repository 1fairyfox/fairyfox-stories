# Principles — the voice this project holds to

- **Craft above all.** Every book is genuinely well written — fully planned, form-true, with a
  real flawed first-person lead and an earned ending. Nothing less than the best we can make.
  If it isn't excellent, it doesn't ship.
- **Plan, then write.** The blueprint maps the whole book before prose begins. No pantsing.
- **The book is the hero, not the collection.** Universes, characters, and cities add meaning
  and cross-linking; they never upstage the individual book or bin it away on the shelf.
- **Simple where it counts.** The *reading* is clean, immersive, uncluttered; depth is story
  and character, never UI clutter or convolution.
- **Range is quality.** The shelf stays wide — genres, tones, forms, narrators — and sometimes
  swings for the creative and unexpected. Sameness is a failure.
- **Transparency & consent.** Full blueprints and content notes up front; no bait-and-switch.
- **Respect the reader.** Their time, their intelligence (subtext over lecture), their privacy
  (local-only progress, no accounts, no tracking, self-hosted fonts).
- **First-class and permanent.** Books are tended over time, not dumped and forgotten.
- **Honest machinery.** Rolls and picks are logged and auditable; the farm doesn't fudge.
- **Mesh discipline.** Living notes kept current, git-flow, accurate legal, standards adopted by
  copy, Jekyll like the hub — same rigour as the rest of fairyfox.io.
