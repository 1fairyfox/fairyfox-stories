# Project Overview

## What It Is

Fairy Fox Stories is an **AI-managed story farm** — a growing library of short interactive
**books** (genuine fiction, fully pre-planned then written a chapter at a time) and, beneath
them, a growing, cross-linked web of **universes, characters, and cities**. It is a public,
contribution-friendly node in the [fairyfox.io](https://fairyfox.io) mesh and a sibling to
`fairyfox-games`: the same disciplined mesh shape (living notes, git-flow, self-hosted fonts,
accurate legal), a different craft — **fiction, not games** — and a **Jekyll** site so the
world meshes as data.

## Goals

- A **wide, ever-deepening shelf** — new books planted (≤ every 3 days), existing books grown
  daily, one whole chapter at a time.
- **Best-in-class craft on every book**, from a 1,500-word short story to a rare 100k novel:
  form-appropriate structure, a real first-person lead with agency, earned endings. No filler.
- **A meshed world:** books belong to universes; characters and cities are first-class,
  cross-linked pages that accumulate as the shelf grows.
- **Full transparency:** every book ships its complete blueprint (open, spoiler-forward) and a
  content note — informed consent before you open it.
- An **immersive, book-like read** in the browser — typographic, drop-capped, progress-tracked
  — with reading progress kept locally only (no accounts, no tracking).
- A living **body of craft knowledge** (`reference/craft/`) the farm writes against and grows.

## How it differs from the games farm

Same mesh discipline; the "unit" is a **book** (blueprint + chapters + book-notes) instead of a
game, plus a **universe/character/city** layer, rendered by **Jekyll** (the games site is
static HTML). Where games *prove* correctness with unit tests, books can't — so integrity
checks cover **structure** (front-matter, chapter counts, cross-references, progress), and
quality is held by the craft notes and review.
