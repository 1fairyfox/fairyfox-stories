# Pitfalls — the newbie traps we never fall into

A checklist of the mistakes that mark amateur fiction. Every one is avoidable with planning
and restraint. Sources: [Erindor Press](https://www.erindorpress.com/2023/05/common-mistakes-new-fiction-writers-make/),
[Kaitlin Slowik](https://kaitlinslowik.com/most-common-mistakes-fiction-authors-make/),
[Globe Soup](https://www.globesoup.net/writing-blog/20-common-fiction-writing-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them).

## Structure & plot

- **Pantsing without a plan.** Diving in with no structure yields aimless narrative,
  inconsistent arcs, and incoherence. *We plant a full blueprint first* — this is the antidote.
- **Events ≠ plot.** A sequence of things happening isn't a story; **cause and effect**
  (causation) is. Each beat should happen *because* of the last. If you can reorder scenes
  freely, the causation is missing.
- **Sagging middle / no escalation.** Stakes must rise. A flat middle bores even with good
  prose. Each turn a little higher than the last.
- **Unearned or cut-to-black endings.** An ending should be traceable to the character's own
  choices. Running out of road and stopping reads as failure.

## Character & POV

- **Flat, faultless leads** (see `character.md`) and **head-hopping** — switching whose head
  we're in without a break — which confuses readers. Hold POV.

## Prose & exposition

- **Telling instead of showing.** Reveal character and plot through action, dialogue, and
  sensory detail — not narrated summary of feelings.
- **Infodumping backstory / worldbuilding.** Large blocks of history dumped at once wreck
  pacing. Release backstory in small, needed pieces, on a need-to-know beat.
- **Purple prose & overwriting.** Adjective pile-ups, strained metaphors, throat-clearing.
  Economy is the style; cut ruthlessly.
- **Weak, on-the-nose, or cheesy dialogue.** People rarely say exactly what they mean; subtext
  carries the scene. No characters announcing the theme.

## Process

- **Editing as you draft.** Trying to polish sentences mid-draft stalls the story; draft the
  chapter, then revise for the bigger picture.
- **Shipping a first draft.** "The End" is a rough draft. A chapter ships only after a real
  revision pass for sense, pacing, and voice.

## Tone (project-specific)

- **Cheesy / cringe / try-hard.** The bar is genuinely well-crafted fiction. No winking
  silliness that undercuts the work, no clichés played straight, no melodrama.

## The pre-ship pass (every chapter)

1. Does each beat follow *causally* from the last?
2. Shown, not told — feelings rendered, not labeled?
3. No infodump; backstory only where earned?
4. POV held; no head-hopping?
5. Dialogue carries subtext; nothing on-the-nose?
6. Cut every line that doesn't earn its place?
