# Prose Style — the spectrum, and rolling one per book

The shelf must not read like one writer with one setting. Each book picks a **prose style on
purpose**, and adjacent books never share one. Style is *chosen* (technique); voice/personality
is *inherent* (`author-personas.md`) — a book needs both. Sources:
[Writer's Digest — voice vs style](https://www.writersdigest.com/write-better-fiction/the-difference-between-voice-and-style-in-writing),
[Well-Storied](https://www.well-storied.com/blog/writing-style-and-voice),
[MasterClass — minimalist/maximalist](https://www.masterclass.com/articles/minimalist-literature-and-maximalist-literature-guide),
[Fiveable — prose style / DIDLS](https://fiveable.me/english-prose-style/unit-1).

## The levers (what you actually vary)

Every style is a *setting* of these dials — set them deliberately, per book:

- **Diction** — word choice: plain vs. ornate, Latinate vs. Anglo-Saxon, formal vs. slangy,
  period vs. modern, concrete vs. abstract. Connotation as much as denotation.
- **Syntax** — sentence architecture: short and clipped vs. long and subordinated; simple vs.
  periodic; fragments; parallelism; how clauses stack.
- **Rhythm & length** — the music: sentence-length variation, punctuation habits (em-dashes,
  semicolons, comma splices, full stops), the pace and breath of a paragraph.
- **Imagery & detail density** — sparse and selective vs. lush and cumulative; which senses;
  how often a figure appears.
- **Figuration** — metaphor/simile: near-none (McCarthy) vs. baroque (Nabokov); fresh vs. plain.
- **Distance & interiority** — close and confiding vs. cool and observational; how much thought
  vs. surface.
- **Dialogue** — clipped and subtextual vs. verbose and witty; dialect; tag habits.
- **Humor & irony** — deadpan, wry, warm, absent, savage.
- **Paragraphing & white space** — dense blocks vs. airy short paragraphs; section breaks.

## A palette of styles (rotate; never two alike back-to-back)

Not a closed list — a starting range. Each is a distinct *setting* of the dials above:

- **Clean minimalist** — Hemingway/Carver/McCarthy: sparse, concrete, subtext-heavy, few
  adjectives, short declaratives; meaning in what's left out.
- **Lyrical / poetic** — rhythmic, image-rich, musical; sentences that swell; emotion in cadence.
- **Maximalist / baroque** — long, subordinated, digressive, dazzling; abundance as the point
  (Faulkner, Pynchon, DFW) — used with control, never as clutter.
- **Plain / journalistic** — transparent, unshowy, reportorial; the prose gets out of the way.
- **Hard-boiled / noir** — terse, cynical, wisecracking, sensory and street-level (our current
  book, *The Cinderwick Job*, leans here).
- **Gothic / ornate** — shadowed, ornamented, dread-laced; heightened diction and atmosphere.
- **Whimsical / comic** — playful, buoyant, digressive-in-a-fun-way; wit foregrounded.
- **Fable / folkloric** — cadenced, timeless, slightly formal; the voice of a told tale.
- **Breathless / propulsive** — present-tense-ish urgency, run-ons, momentum over polish.
- **Cool / ironic literary** — precise, restrained, observational, dry.
- **Ornamented vernacular** — voice-forward, dialect- or idiom-rich, musical everyday speech.
- **Epistolary / documentary** — letters, logs, transcripts, found-text collage.

## How the farm uses it

- **Roll a style on planting** (`operating-model.md`), coherent with the book's genre, form,
  and author-persona — but deliberately **different from recent books**. Record it in the
  blueprint + book-notes.
- **Match, don't clash.** Style serves the story: a caper wants pace (hard-boiled/propulsive);
  a haunted tale wants gothic/lyrical; a quiet character piece wants minimalist or cool-literary.
- **Hold it for the whole book.** The chosen style is consistent chapter to chapter — the
  book-notes `style` entry is the reference every chapter is written against.
- **Range is a quality goal.** Two books in a row that read the same is a failure, even if both
  are good. Vary the dials, not just the plot.

## Guardrails

- **Every style is held to the craft bar.** Maximalist ≠ purple; minimalist ≠ thin; experimental
  ≠ sloppy (`what-makes-fiction-good.md`, `pitfalls.md`).
- **Style ≠ gimmick.** An unusual style must earn its keep by fitting the story, not decorate it.
- **First-person + a real MC still holds** across every style.
