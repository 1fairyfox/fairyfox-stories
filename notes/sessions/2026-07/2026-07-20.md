## Written — ch.2 "The Ear of the House" (~1,930 words, book at 2/7)

Written straight to the blueprint's chapter map (the road east · three laws · Noor's ear · the opened
town · the wind's doubleness) — no arc deviation:

- **Opens off "I"** (voice guard held) — on the weight of a carried vs. chased wind, then the road and
  the child bracing against the empty air like a boat-rail; her first gust ("the flat ground took a
  breath"), the two fingers to her throat.
- **The three laws in showman's patter.** (1) naming (restated). (2) **Proving law — the ch.7 hook,
  planted verbatim:** proved once at the buyer's own threshold; tied to the contract phrase *bottled
  and delivered* → "the proving is the delivering. No threshold, no sale." (3) flavour: "never sell a
  wind you'd miss," which **Noor re-reads flat** — "That is not a law of the craft. That is a law about
  you." (foil's first true blow; seeds the stopper/Nuru theme unspoken).
- **Noor's ear made concrete + trained** (not wise-child magic): raised in the vault among the
  ninety-nine knocking against glass at night; her private names — the **Sigher** (Kessa dawn wind),
  the **Presser** (Talibar grief wind), the **Little Heresy** (ch.1's lamp-snuffer, now knocking west).
  "They want out … now they only ever knock." Her want plain: "I have never once been in weather … I
  did not know it would feel like a hand."
- **The opened town** teaches the doubleness by example: read the Unbottled's passage by *openness*, not
  wreckage. Mischief (a nine-year marriage broken by a blown-open letter) set beside judgment (the
  grain-factor's filed short-weight scale aired on the cobbles, clearing a long-dead girl blamed for
  his theft — her name given back). Thesis line: "It doesn't choose … It only opens. What comes out is
  the town's, not the wind's."
- **Stopper beats held** — his hand goes to the cracked horn twice (at the fire; in the opened town,
  "every shut thing but the one I carried"), name still unspoken. **Guarded:** Emir's grief (ch.5),
  Nuru's kinship (ch.6). **Escalation set:** the wind is west, does not yet know his scent — ch.3's
  failed mastery-try is what turns it onto his road; close line points straight at it.

Book-notes gained a **"Ch.2 — new canon"** section (the three laws' verbatim seeds, Noor's ear/wind
