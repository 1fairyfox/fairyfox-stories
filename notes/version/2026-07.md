## 0.1.0 — Bootstrap the story farm (greenfield)

Set Fairy Fox Stories up from scratch as an AI story farm, per the hub `new-project-setup`
runbook (collection/monorepo exception, greenfield after clearing a prior unauthorized
scaffold). Adopted the hub standards + living-notes system. Wrote the **craft body of
knowledge** (forms, structure, character, first-person voice, pitfalls, what-makes-fiction-good,
serialization, genre range) and the farm's operating model, universe system, and
reading-experience specs. Decided the architecture: **Jekyll** site, **form-first** books,
comprehensive **universe/character/city** collections, first-person real-character mandate,
OpenAI for prose/art. Built the Jekyll scaffold and planted book #1 with its first chapter.
Verified the shelf and reader in Chrome (fixed shelf-card sizing so books stay book-sized; drop
cap + progress confirmed), and set the content bar: **dark but broadly accessible — no explicit/
gore/obscenity** ("dark but not adult-only"). Retired the grief-themed book #1 and planted
**_The Cinderwick Job_** (a caper in the new Gaslight Reach universe) in its place; sharpened the
tone bar to **entertaining-dark, not bleak**. Added a **style + author-persona system**
(`craft/style-spectrum.md`, `craft/author-personas.md`) so every book rolls its own prose style
and a distinct passionate "author" — the shelf reads like a collaboration of many writers. Made
**pseudo-authors a first-class, meshed collection** (`_authors`): planting rolls new-vs-existing
author (like the universe roll), books carry an `author` byline linking to the author's page, and
author pages auto-list their body of work — a mesh even between pseudo-authors. First author:
**Del Marsh** (the wry street-poet behind *The Cinderwick Job*).
