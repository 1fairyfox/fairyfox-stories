# Changelog — 2026-07

Newest first. Plain-English, per substantive commit. No version bump = maintenance.

## 0.2.2 — A face for the farm (site emblem + social cards)

Put the new Stories emblem (`assets/icon.png` — an open book sprouting a plant over a shelved
library) to work in the three places it was missing. Added it to the **landing masthead** beside
the title, reusing the theme's existing `.mast-logo` slot with an `.emblem` treatment in
`home.css` so the tall, transparent art shows whole instead of being clipped to the round avatar.
Wired it into **jekyll-seo-tag** as the site `logo` (JSON-LD publisher logo) and as the default
social share card (`image`) for every page — previously nothing set `image`, so shared links had
no preview; books, which use a custom `cover:` field seo-tag doesn't read, now fall back to the
emblem too. Left the shared Fairy Fox **hub fox** in the site header/footer and the root favicons
untouched (cross-project branding). `npm test` green; `jekyll build` clean; masthead previewed
desktop + mobile.

## 0.2.1 — The farm actually runs now (daily automation)

The grow/plant cadence was written down but nothing ran it, so the shelf sat still after v0.2.0.
Added a scheduled daily job (midnight, America/Denver) that **grows 2 books every day** and
**plants a new book once 3 days have passed**, then **auto-publishes to the live site**. Set the
grow count to **2** (was "up to 5") in the operating model — single source of truth — and recorded
the automation + auto-publish decision. Notes + config only; no reader-facing change in this
commit (the chapters it writes land on later runs).

## 0.2.0 — Five books, with art (the shelf comes alive)

Planted the starting shelf: **five books, each a distinct genre, form, style, and author** — the
caper *The Cinderwick Job* (Del Marsh), the cosy magical-realism *The Two-O'Clock Launderette*
(Posy Fell), the SF *The Cartographer of Decks* (Idris Okonkwo-Vance), the gothic *The Wintering
House* (Cordelia Fenn), and the fable *The Girl Who Sold the Wind* (Amara Okri) — each with a full
public blueprint, chapter 1, its own new universe, and its author-persona. **Generated cover + a
chapter-1 header image for every book** via the OpenAI image tooling (gpt-image-1, ~$0.04 each);
made the tooling model-aware and budget-guarded. UI: masthead now reads "An AI-managed story farm —
new books are planted, and the ones already here keep growing," added an **Authors** count chip,
and cut the shelf-card cover height ~25% (square covers); chapter headers render as banners.

## 0.1.0 — Bootstrap the story farm (greenfield)

Set Fairy Fox Stories up from scratch as an AI story farm, per the hub `new-project-setup`
runbook (collection/monorepo exception, greenfield after clearing a prior unauthorized
scaffold). Adopted the hub standards + living-notes system. Wrote the **craft body of
knowledge** (forms, structure, character, first-person voice, pitfalls, what-makes-fiction-good,
serialization, genre range) and the farm's operating model, universe system, and
reading-experience specs. Decided the architecture: **Jekyll** site, **form-first** books,
comprehensive **universe/character/city** collections, first-person real-character mandate,
OpenAI for prose/art. Built the Jekyll scaffold and planted book #1 with its first chapter.
Verified the shelf and reader in Chrome (fixed shelf-card sizing so books stay book-sized; drop
cap + progress confirmed), and set the content bar: **dark but broadly accessible — no explicit/
gore/obscenity** ("dark but not adult-only"). Retired the grief-themed book #1 and planted
**_The Cinderwick Job_** (a caper in the new Gaslight Reach universe) in its place; sharpened the
tone bar to **entertaining-dark, not bleak**. Added a **style + author-persona system**
(`craft/style-spectrum.md`, `craft/author-personas.md`) so every book rolls its own prose style
and a distinct passionate "author" — the shelf reads like a collaboration of many writers. Made
**pseudo-authors a first-class, meshed collection** (`_authors`): planting rolls new-vs-existing
author (like the universe roll), books carry an `author` byline linking to the author's page, and
author pages auto-list their body of work — a mesh even between pseudo-authors. First author:
**Del Marsh** (the wry street-poet behind *The Cinderwick Job*).
